Retention & Difficulty Correlation

Key insights derived from model features

Restart Rate is a Critical Churn Signal

High restart rates (sum of remaining steps `f_reststep`) are highly correlated with churn, signifying frustration or high level difficulty.

Help Items Mitigate Early Churn

Players who leverage the help function (`f_help`) when struggling have a 34% higher retention rate than those who restart repeatedly without help.

The "Sweet Spot" Playtime

The optimal player playtime window that yields the highest retention is between 120 and 300 minutes, after which player churn stabilizes.

Project Overview

The Player Engagement & Retention Analytics System is a data-driven prediction platform designed to analyze play patterns, predict churn risks, and dynamically prescribe customer-retention campaigns for puzzle gaming applications.

Dataset Details

The underlying data consists of raw player level sequence traces containing 2.19 million attempts across 1,509 unique levels, including attempt durations, move efficiencies, hints utilized, and temporal sequence timestamps.

Feature Engineering

Generates 14 distinct player behavior metrics (e.g., success rates, restart frequencies, item-help rates, playtimes) merged with baseline level difficulties (global average retry times and level pass rates).

Model Metrics

Trained and validated candidate classifiers including Decision Trees, Logistic Regression, XGBoost, and Random Forest. Deployed the Random Forest ensemble model for low-bias predictions.

Technology Stack

Built using python FastAPI backends loading joblib objects, served onto standard Vanilla HTML, CSS, JavaScript web layouts rendering charts via ApexCharts.

Business Insights

Enables live retention interventions: high risk players are flagged for booster packs or targeted discount campaigns, while highly engaged players receive loyalty rewards, streaks, and milestones.
